---
title: Recording
description: How to record meetings with openNotes
---

openNotes captures audio from your microphone or system audio and transcribes it in real-time.

## Starting a recording

1. Open openNotes and navigate to the **Record** tab
2. Select your **microphone** from the device dropdown
3. Click the **Record** button to begin

The app starts transcribing immediately — you'll see text appearing in real-time as speech is detected.

## Microphone selection

openNotes lists all available audio input devices on your system. Choose the microphone that best captures meeting audio:

- **Built-in microphone** — Works for in-person meetings at your computer
- **External USB microphone** — Better audio quality, recommended for important meetings
- **Virtual audio device** — Capture system audio from video calls (e.g., BlackHole on macOS, VB-Cable on Windows)

:::tip
For remote meetings on platforms like Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet, use a virtual audio device to capture the meeting audio directly from your system rather than from your speakers.
:::

You can set a preferred microphone in **Settings** so it's automatically selected each time.

## During recording

While recording, you'll see:

- **Live transcript** — Text updates in real-time as speech is detected
- **Recording indicator** — Shows that audio is being captured
- **Timer** — Displays the recording duration

The floating recording widget remains visible even when you switch to other apps, so you always know when recording is active.

## Stopping a recording

Click the **Stop** button to end the recording. openNotes will:

1. Finalize the transcript
2. Automatically generate a summary using your configured AI model
3. Save the meeting to your library
4. Navigate to the summary view

## Tips for better recordings

- **Minimize background noise** — Close windows, mute notifications, use a quiet room
- **Speak clearly** — The transcription model works best with clear speech at a moderate pace
- **Use a good microphone** — External microphones significantly improve transcription accuracy
- **Position your mic correctly** — Keep it close to the speaker(s) for best results
